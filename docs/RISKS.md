# Risks

Status: authoritative for this phase. Risk register per
`MASTER_SPEC.md`'s brief. Impact and probability are rated **Low / Medium /
High** at the architecture-design stage (pre-implementation); revisit with
real data once phases in [`IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md`](IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md)
ship.

| #   | Risk                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        | Impact | Probability                                                                                                  | Mitigation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Medical/safety risk** — the app recommends an exercise or load inappropriate for a user's actual physical condition, or fails to escalate concerning pain appropriately.                                                                                                                                                                                                                  | High   | Medium                                                                                                       | Layer 1 deterministic safety rules gate all exercise eligibility independent of AI (`AI_ARCHITECTURE.md` §2.1); pain is a dedicated non-diagnostic flow with conservative default actions (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §8.2); MVP positioning explicitly excludes clinical/rehab use and requires professional-clearance flags where screening indicates (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §3, §8.1); safety tests are a named, mandatory test category in every phase touching Layer 1/2 (`IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` Phase 7). Residual risk: self-reported screening can be incomplete or dishonest; no architecture eliminates that — product/legal copy must set expectations clearly (see `OPEN_QUESTIONS.md`).                        |
| 2   | **AI hallucination** — the Coach states something false or ungrounded (a fabricated statistic, an invented safety claim, a claim contradicting the deterministic engine's actual reasoning).                                                                                                                                                                                                | High   | Medium                                                                                                       | The LLM never originates programme/safety decisions (Layer 6 boundary, `AI_ARCHITECTURE.md` §2.6); every factual claim must trace to `StructuredContext` (§4), checked by an automated groundedness pass in the evaluation harness (§10) before any prompt/provider change ships. Residual risk: conversational rephrasing can still subtly misstate a grounded fact; groundedness checks reduce but do not fully eliminate this — human review remains part of the evaluation loop.                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| 3   | **BodyScan accuracy / false precision** — users trust an image-derived number (measurement, "percentage to goal") that the underlying method cannot actually support.                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | Medium | Medium (High if computer vision ships without discipline)                                                    | MVP BodyScan is explicitly comparison imagery only, no automated measurement extraction (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §23); Scan Reliability Score (P1) is required to gate any future automated use, with "Low reliability → never drives major automated change" as a hard rule (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §23.4); trajectory language is categorical, never a fabricated percentage (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §26.1), enforced by a content-lint test (`IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` Phase 10).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| 4   | **Privacy breach of body imagery** — BodyScan images exposed to the wrong user, a third party, or used for a purpose the user didn't consent to.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            | High   | Low (if architecture is followed)                                                                            | Private storage bucket, no public URLs, signed URLs scoped to the requesting owner only (`ARCHITECTURE.md` §9); explicit per-purpose consent (`consent_records`) before any capture; hard "no ad use, no sale, no external model training on identifiable images without separate explicit consent" rule (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §25). Residual risk: implementation bugs in signed-URL scoping or storage policy — mitigated by dedicated automated storage-privacy tests (`IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` Phase 10), not just RLS on the database rows.                                                                                                                                                                   |
| 5   | **RLS misconfiguration** — a policy gap lets one user read/write another user's data.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | High   | Medium (RLS is easy to get subtly wrong, e.g. missing policy on a new table, an overly broad `USING` clause) | RLS enabled on every user-owned table with no exceptions is a stated hard rule (`ARCHITECTURE.md` §8, `DATABASE_SCHEMA.md`); a negative RLS test is required per table starting Phase 2, re-run after every schema change through Phase 11's full audit (`IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md`); CI should fail (implementation-time decision) if a new table lacks a corresponding RLS test.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| 6   | **Offline synchronisation bugs** — a workout is lost, duplicated, or corrupted during the offline → sync transition.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        | High   | Medium                                                                                                       | Local-first-then-sync design with idempotent client-generated UUIDs (`ARCHITECTURE.md` §5) specifically to make retried syncs safe; dedicated offline-simulation integration tests are a named Phase 6 deliverable. Residual risk: storage cleared/app reinstalled mid-sync is an accepted, documented limitation (`ARCHITECTURE.md` §5.1) — genuinely unrecoverable, not merely untested; user-facing sync-status visibility (§5.1) at least prevents the user being _unknowingly_ at risk.                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| 7   | **Over-personalisation / runaway adaptation** — the system changes a user's programme too often on weak signal, destroying progression tracking and trust.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  | Medium | Medium                                                                                                       | This is the exact failure mode the Evidence & Confidence Engine and stability windows exist to prevent (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §15–16, `PROGRAMME_ENGINE.md` §8); explicit automated tests assert a single weak signal cannot trigger a Level 2+ change (`IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` Phase 7). Residual risk: thresholds are initially estimated, not empirically tuned — flagged as a known limitation, to be revisited with real post-launch data (`DEFERRED.md` — "more sophisticated adaptation," P1).                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| 8   | **Body-image harm** — the product's language, imagery, or mechanics (scoring, comparison, streaks) cause or worsen body-image distress or disordered behaviour.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | High   | Medium                                                                                                       | Hard language rules enforced by a content-lint test across UI copy and AI output (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §4, §20.1); no attractiveness scoring or cross-user comparison, ever, as an architectural rule (§4); non-destructive Momentum/streak model (§17); no daily-scan pressure, cadence guidance of 2–4 weeks (§23.5); explicit non-diagnostic stance on disordered patterns, with supportive/escalation copy rather than app-driven diagnosis. Residual risk: this is a product-copy and ongoing-judgement risk, not something architecture alone resolves — flagged for continued review, not a one-time gate.                                                                                                  |
| 9   | **AI/model cost overrun** — Coach usage scales cost unpredictably, or a bug causes runaway LLM calls.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | Medium | Medium                                                                                                       | Deterministic-first cost ordering (`AI_ARCHITECTURE.md` §11); per-user per-operation rate limiting at the Edge Function layer (`ARCHITECTURE.md` §12); per-request context-size budgets; usage/cost logging tied to observability from Phase 9 onward. Residual risk: rate limits must be tuned against real usage; initial values are estimates.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| 10  | **Feature creep / scope violation of `CLAUDE.md`** — building later phases early, or silently simplifying/dropping a requirement.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           | Medium | Medium (a common failure mode on ambitious specs like this one)                                              | `IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md`'s explicit non-goals per phase; `CLAUDE.md`'s "work one phase at a time" and "never silently remove or ignore a requirement" are treated as binding; every deferral is recorded in `DEFERRED.md` rather than dropped; every disagreement with a requirement is recorded in `DECISIONS.md`/here rather than quietly resolved by omission.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| 11  | **App-store / regulatory risk** — health/fitness claims, data collection (especially imagery + health screening data), or subscription mechanics run afoul of App Store/Play Store review or regional health-data regulation (e.g. treating BodyScan/health screening data with a rigour approaching health-data regulation even though the product is not positioned as a medical device). | Medium | Medium                                                                                                       | MVP explicitly avoids medical/clinical positioning and diagnostic claims (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §3, §8.2, §23.5); privacy architecture (consent, deletion, export, no ad use of body imagery) is built to a standard that anticipates scrutiny rather than minimal compliance (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §25); subscription architecture avoids paywalling safety (§30). Residual risk: specific regional regulatory requirements (health-data-specific consent language, age-gating specifics, biometric-data laws applicable to body imagery in some jurisdictions) are not fully enumerated in this architecture phase — flagged as needing dedicated legal/compliance review before public launch (`OPEN_QUESTIONS.md`). |
| 12  | **Exercise-data quality** — an incorrect or poorly-tagged exercise (wrong muscle mapping, wrong equipment requirement, missing restriction flag) propagates into unsafe or irrelevant recommendations at scale, since selection is data-driven.                                                                                                                                             | Medium | Medium                                                                                                       | Exercise selection is explicitly structured-data-driven, not LLM-improvised, which makes bad data auditable and fixable in one place rather than diffused through model behaviour (`MASTER_SPEC.md` §9); Phase 4 requires referential-integrity and completeness validation tests before the library is considered seeded; a content-authoring process (not just a one-time seed script) is a stated Phase 4 deliverable so corrections don't require a code deploy. Residual risk: correctness of the _content itself_ (is this really a safe regression for that restriction?) depends on subject-matter review, which is a content/product process this architecture phase does not itself perform.         |
| 13  | **Single-writer offline assumption breaks in practice** — a user genuinely does use two devices for the same workout (e.g. phone dies mid-workout, switches to a tablet), and MVP's single-writer design produces confusing state.                                                                                                                                                          | Low    | Low–Medium                                                                                                   | Explicitly documented as an accepted MVP limitation (`DEFERRED.md`); the failure mode is "confusing, not data-corrupting" because idempotent per-workout writes still can't double-count a set, only potentially interleave oddly across devices — worth monitoring via support/analytics rather than solving speculatively pre-launch.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| 14  | **Cold-start personalisation gap** — before enough evidence exists, Programme Fit-adjacent and preference-driven scoring effectively degrades to the non-personalised baseline, which may read as "the app doesn't seem smart yet" to early users.                                                                                                                                          | Low    | High (true for every new user, by definition)                                                                | Treated as expected behaviour, not a defect — `PROGRAMME_ENGINE.md` §3.2 explicitly scores a missing preference signal as neutral rather than guessing; Progress screens show "insufficient evidence" as a first-class, non-alarming state (`SCREEN_SPECIFICATIONS.md` §6) rather than hiding the gap. Mitigation is expectation-setting in product copy (Programme Preview, `SCREEN_SPECIFICATIONS.md` §2), not a technical fix.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |

## Notes on methodology

This register reflects risks visible at the architecture/specification
stage. It does not replace a security review, a legal/compliance review, or
a clinical-safety review of actual exercise content — those are follow-on
activities this document explicitly flags rather than substitutes for
(see [`OPEN_QUESTIONS.md`](OPEN_QUESTIONS.md)). Update this register at the
end of each `IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` phase with what was actually learned,
not just what was anticipated here.
